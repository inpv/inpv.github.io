About

I've been into computers since I can remember myself.

Back in the day, like many, I was fascinated by the book "I was in the computer city" and later "Professor Fortran's Encyclopedia", also LEGO, a lot of it.

Still, PCs were quite rare and I didn't have much actual experience beyond launching video games in the MS-DOS environment on an i486.

Only way after, when I decided to buy the Nokia N9 as my main smartphone, did I discover the wonderful world of Linux.

So I started programming for myself, first in Pascal. You can see one of my earlier Pascal projects rewritten into Python here.

But it still wasn't enough for me, I wanted to work in the tech industry full time, so I got into SPbStU university and got a postgraduate degree from the faculty of Mechanics and Mathematics, while working as a web admin and PM.

Then I decided to get into QA engineering, because I always liked dissecting stuff and always had a keen eye for inconsistencies.

Hope the content of this blog will be of any use for whoever searches for solutions of software problems.
